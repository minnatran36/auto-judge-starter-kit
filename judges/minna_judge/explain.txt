------------------------------------------------
If two ground truth measures rank responses differently from each other, then no single eval measure can have kendall = 1 against both simultaneously. This is just an algebraic fact: a single ranking can match at most one target ranking exactly.

The best a single judge can do against multiple truth measures is land somewhere on the Pareto frontier — the set of points where you can't improve correlation with one truth measure without sacrificing correlation with another.

A judge that has kendall = 1 against every truth measure can only exist if all truth measures are themselves perfectly correlated with each other. In your data, they're not. So that judge cannot exist.
